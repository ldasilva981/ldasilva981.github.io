Galinhada

History

Galinhada is a traditional Brazilian dish. It is chicken cooked with rice and several tasty seasonings. The dish started with Portuguese expeditioners who were searching for gold in Minas Gerais. It has evolved and is now strongly associated with typical Brazilian cuisine. It is often served on Holy Saturday to celebrate the end of Lent. The word galinhada originates from galinha which means chicken. It is cooked in one big pot and is known to be a filling food for a lot of people.

This recipe is simple and easy. Make sure to have all the ingrediants ready before jumping in to actually make it. It will feed a lot of people and will make a tasty Galinhada!

Ingredients:

  • 1 Chicken
  • 4 Potatoes
  • Garlic
    • 8 Cloves
  • Full head of onion
  • 1 Cup of Tomato Sauce
  • Parsley
  • Chives
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Vegetable Oil
  • White Rice

Directions:

  1. Wash your chicken and cut into smaller peices
  2. Peel your four potatoes and cut into cubes
  3. Get a medium sized pot and heat it to medium heat
  4. Add all seasoning into pot:
    • Sliced onions
    • 1 cup of tomato sauce
    • Full spoon of salt
    • Full spoon of pepper
    • Generous amount of vegetable oil
  5. Let the seasoning cook for three minutes
  6. Add chicken and potato
  7. Mix and cook for 10 minutes
  8. Add 2 full cups of rice
  9. Mix untill fully marinated
  10. Throw in 3 cups of boiling hot water
  11. Shut the pot with a top
  12. Cook for 20 minutes
  13. Enjoy!
